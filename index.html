8. Non-WLA Signal QPPV assesses Ongoing Assessment report 180-day report to NPC

Saudi-Specific Pharmacovigilance Requirements (SFDA GVP v3.2)

Personnel & Systems

  • QPPV Registration: Full-time local QPPV + Deputy via Saudi Vigilance System.
  • QPPV Training: Every 3 years (was 2) + must be licensed by SCFHS.
  • Arabic Webpage: Mandatory for ADR reporting with QPPV contact information.

Documents & Submissions

  • SSA (Saudi-Specific Annex): Required with every RMP submission (pre- and post-approval).
  • PSUR/PBRER: Submit via CD or DS@sfda.gov.sa + provide an annual submission plan.
  • aRMMs: Submit Word & Designed versions + Translator License (for Arabic translation).

Communications & Signals

  • DHPC: One MAH (preferably originator) acts as the single SFDA contact point.
  • Signal Timelines (WHO-WLA): 21 days to submit a variation if SPC/PIL change is needed.
  • Signal Timelines (Non-WLA): 180 days to submit an assessment report to the NPC.

Key Saudi PV Reporting Timelines

ASAP (24h)

ICSRs (Serious, KSA): Report all serious adverse reactions occurring in Saudi Arabia.

(Previously 15 days, now aligned with global best practice)

21 Days

WHO-WLA Signals: Submit a variation to SFDA if a signal from a WHO-WLA country requires a change to the SPC or PIL.

30-60 Days

IND/CTA Review: SFDA review period for Clinical Trial Applications.

180 Days

Non-WLA Signals: Submit a full assessment report to the National Pharmacovigilance Center (NPC) for any new signals.

3 Years

QPPV Training: Refresher training and competency assessment for the local QPPV (updated from 2 years).

Ongoing

PSUR/PBRER: Submitted according to the approved annual plan and product lifecycle.
